The International Isotope Society UK Group

Code of Conduct

The International Isotope Society (UK Group) is committed to providing a professional, respectful, and inclusive environment for all participants in its activities. This Code of Conduct applies to all IIS UK events, meetings, communications, and online platforms.

Expected Behaviour

All participants are expected to:

  • Treat others with respect, professionalism, and courtesy.
  • Communicate in a constructive and inclusive manner.
  • Be mindful of diversity and differing perspectives.
  • Follow applicable health, safety, and organisational guidelines.

Unacceptable Behaviour

The following behaviours will not be tolerated:

  • Harassment, discrimination, or intimidation of any kind.
  • Offensive or inappropriate language or conduct.
  • Disruptive behaviour that negatively impacts others’ participation.
  • Any form of bullying, exclusion, or inappropriate conduct.

Reporting Concerns

If you experience or witness behaviour that violates this Code of Conduct, . All reports will be treated seriously and confidentially where possible.

Consequences

Failure to comply with this Code of Conduct may result in:

  • Removal from events or activities
  • Suspension of membership or participation
  • Further actions as deemed appropriate by the IIS UK Committee

Diversity, Equality & Inclusion (DEI) Statement

At the International Isotope Society (UK Group), we are committed to fostering a diverse, inclusive, and equitable scientific community. We recognise that innovation and excellence in isotope science are strengthened by the inclusion of individuals from varied backgrounds, experiences, and perspectives. We actively support equal participation and opportunity across all aspects of our activities, including membership, events and leadership.

We are committed to:

  • Promoting equality of opportunity regardless of age, disability, gender identity, race, ethnicity, religion, sexual orientation, or socioeconomic background
  • Creating inclusive and respectful environments at all IIS UK events and activities
  • Encouraging participation from underrepresented groups within the isotope and wider scientific community
  • Supporting early-career scientists and those from diverse backgrounds through networking and mentorship.

We expect all members, partners, and participants in IIS UK activities to uphold these principles and contribute to a positive and inclusive community.
